Colorado Outdoor Adventures

Estes Park

Nestled at the eastern gateway of Rocky Mountain National Park, Estes Park is a mountain town celebrated for its breathtaking alpine scenery, abundant wildlife, and year-round adventure. Visitors can travel the Trail Ridge Road, the highest continuous paved highway in North America, to enjoy panoramic views of snowcapped peaks and valleys teeming with elk and bighorn sheep. Downtown Estes Park offers a charming mix of local shops, art galleries, and riverside dining, while the historic Stanley Hotel, built in 1909 and famously inspiring Stephen King's The Shining, adds a touch of mystery and grandeur. Outdoor enthusiasts can hike, kayak on Lake Estes, or go fly-fishing along the Big Thompson River, while the annual Elk Fest celebrates the town's connection to nature. With its blend of natural beauty, small-town charm, and access to some of Colorado's most stunning landscapes, Estes Park stands as a quintessential mountain getaway.

Colorado Springs

Situated at the base of Pikes Peak, one of Colorado's most iconic fourteeners, Colorado Springs offers a remarkable blend of natural beauty, history, and adventure. The city's crown jewel, Garden of the Gods, features towering red rock formations ideal for hiking, photography, and rock climbing. Visitors can ascend Pikes Peak via the Cog Railway or scenic Pikes Peak Highway, both offering awe-inspiring views that inspired the song America the Beautiful. The city also boasts attractions like the Broadmoor Seven Falls, Cave of the Winds Mountain Park, and the U.S. Air Force Academy, known for its striking Cadet Chapel. Historic Old Colorado City preserves the charm of the Gold Rush era with galleries, boutiques, and restaurants, while annual events like the Pikes Peak International Hill Climb and Colorado Balloon Classic reflect the city's adventurous spirit. With its perfect mix of outdoor recreation, cultural landmarks, and historic character, Colorado Springs remains one of the state's most dynamic destinations.

Other Notable Places in Colorado

Beyond its famous mountain towns, Colorado offers an extraordinary variety of destinations that showcase its diverse geography and culture. In southern Colorado, Great Sand Dunes National Park and Preserve features North America's tallest dunes, rising up to 750 feet against the stunning backdrop of the Sangre de Cristo Mountains. To the west, Grand Junction and the Grand Mesa—the world's largest flat-top mountain—offer breathtaking red rock canyons, alpine lakes, and scenic trails. Durango charms visitors with its historic Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad, while Crested Butte, known as the “Wildflower Capital of Colorado,” transforms into a vibrant paradise of color each summer and a premier ski destination in winter. Aspen combines world-class skiing with cultural sophistication, home to music and film festivals and the stunning Maroon Bells peaks. Meanwhile, Fort Collins delights with its craft beer culture and lively downtown, and Pueblo highlights Colorado's Hispanic heritage along its scenic Riverwalk. Together, these destinations reveal Colorado's incredible range—from desert dunes and alpine forests to thriving cities steeped in history and creativity.
